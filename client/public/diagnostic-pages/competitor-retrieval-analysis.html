The Core Question: Why Them, Not You?

Both you and your competitor rank in the search results for the same query. Both have relevant content. But when ChatGPT or Perplexity generates an answer, it cites the competitor and ignores you.

This is not luck. Models have systematic reasons for source preference. Understanding those reasons—and extracting them from CITE LEDGER—is what separates diagnostic thinking from guessing.

The difference:

Seven Source Preference Vectors Models Use

Models evaluate competitor vs. you along these dimensions. Each is ledger-traceable:

1. Author Authority Vector

What models measure: Explicit author credentials, publication history, cited mentions in other authoritative sources.

Ledger evidence:

  • Your page: No author listed, or generic "by the Marketing Team"
  • Competitor: Author is a recognized researcher (10+ published papers cited in Google Scholar), credentials in page schema
  • Model decision: Competitor wins authority gate

Forensic marker: CITE LEDGER shows "author_authority_comparison" with numeric scores

2. Citation Network Vector

What models measure: How many authoritative sources cite this page? Do other trusted pages link to it with credible anchor text?

Ledger evidence:

  • Your page: 3 backlinks, all from blogs
  • Competitor: 47 backlinks, including from .edu domains, government sources, academic journals
  • Model decision: Competitor page is validated by the ecosystem you're not part of

Forensic marker: "citation_network_density" with backlink source classification

3. Semantic Consistency Vector

What models measure: Does this page's claims align with consensus information from other sources the model trusts?

Ledger evidence:

  • Your page: Claims "AI models can be 100% objective" (contradicts authoritative sources)
  • Competitor: Claims "AI models have inherent biases in training data" (aligns with published research consensus)
  • Model decision: Competitor page passes the consistency verification gate

Forensic marker: "semantic_consistency_score" + "consensus_alignment"

4. Content Structure Extraction Vector

What models measure: Can the page be cleanly parsed into quotable blocks? Is the information structured for extraction?

Ledger evidence:

  • Your page: Long prose paragraphs, no FAQ schema, no definition blocks, mixed formatting
  • Competitor: FAQPage schema with clear Q&A, definition lists, bulleted summaries at strategic points
  • Model decision: Competitor page is extraction-optimized; model can quote cleanly

Forensic marker: "extraction_structure_score" with schema analysis

5. Recency and Freshness Vector

What models measure: When was this page last updated? Does it reference current data?

Ledger evidence:

  • Your page: Last updated 18 months ago; references 2024 statistics
  • Competitor: Updated 2 weeks ago; references 2026 research published this quarter
  • Model decision: For current events or trending topics, competitor is preferred

Forensic marker: "content_freshness_score" with date analysis + data recency flagging

6. Domain Authority Reputation Vector

What models measure: What's the track record of this domain? Has it published misinformation before? Is it well-established?

Ledger evidence:

  • Your page: Domain 3 years old, mixed history (some pages fact-checked with errors)
  • Competitor: Domain 15+ years old, established reputation, no significant misinformation incidents
  • Model decision: Competitor domain is trusted based on historical track record

Forensic marker: "domain_reputation_score" with misinformation incident count

7. Query Intent Alignment Vector

What models measure: How precisely does this page match the specific user intent (informational vs. commercial vs. navigational)?

Ledger evidence:

  • Your page: Generic guide on the topic; covers multiple angles
  • Competitor: Precisely answers the specific question in user query; narrow scope, deep depth
  • Model decision: For a specific query, competitor is the better match

Forensic marker: "query_intent_alignment" score + semantic matching depth

Strategic Gap Types and Remediation

Structural Gaps (Low Effort, High Impact)

  • Gap: You have prose; competitor has FAQPage schema
  • Fix: 4–6 hours to add FAQ schema to top 5 pages
  • Expected impact: +15–20 points on extraction structure vector

Attribution Gaps (Medium Effort, High Impact)

  • Gap: Competitor lists 3+ author credentials; you list 0
  • Fix: 2–3 days to implement author schema, bylines, bio pages
  • Expected impact: +25–30 points on author authority vector

Citation Network Gaps (High Effort, Medium Impact)

  • Gap: Competitor has 40 backlinks from .edu/.gov; you have 5
  • Fix: 4–8 weeks of outreach, content collaboration, guest features
  • Expected impact: +10–15 points on citation network vector over 2 months

Reputation Gaps (Long-term, Medium Impact)

  • Gap: Your domain is newer with mixed history; competitor is established 15+ years
  • Fix: 6–12 months of consistent, high-quality publishing + zero misinformation incidents
  • Expected impact: +5–10 points on domain reputation vector (over time)
