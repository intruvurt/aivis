Entity Decay Over Time: When AI Models Lose Trust in Your Brand

Real-time forensic timeline of how entities disappear from model synthesis.

The Pattern: Your Citation Presence Collapses Without Warning

Your brand was cited by ChatGPT. Then Perplexity stopped mentioning you. Then Google AI Overviews removed you from synthesis. All in 2–3 weeks.

This is not a technical failure. This is entity decay—the pattern where AI models systematically downgrade trust in an entity over time and route user queries toward competitors instead.

Unlike traditional search:

  • Google updates: Happen monthly, predictable, tied to algorithm rollouts
  • Model decay: Happens continuously, triggered by LLM retraining, conflicting information, or competitor author claims

Google decay is SEO. Model decay is reputation degradation.

Three Types of Entity Decay

Type 1: Authority Reassignment Decay

Pattern: Your entity was cited for topic X. A new competitor claims authority over topic X. Models gradually shift future queries to the competitor.

Cause: The model encounters the competitor's content multiple times, each time with stronger authority signals (more citations from established sources, published in recognized publications, etc.). Model retraining or LoRA tuning shifts the entity association.

Timeline: 1–3 weeks from first competitor emergence

Forensic Signal:

  • CITE LEDGER shows competitor gaining retrieval priority
  • Your authority score doesn't drop; citations just route to competitor instead
  • You're still retrieved, but ranked lower in synthesis candidates

Type 2: Conflicting Information Decay

Pattern: Your content contradicts information the model learned from authoritative sources during pre-training. As models update or encounter conflicting evidence, they deprioritize you and use the authoritative source instead.

Cause: You make a claim the model's reasoning-chain later finds contradicted by authoritative sources (Wikipedia, academic citations, official government posts).

Timeline: Days to weeks (faster than authority reassignment)

Forensic Signal:

  • CITE LEDGER shows semantic inconsistency flag
  • Your authority score may actually be good, but synthesis gates reject you on verification
  • Competitor cited instead, marker shows "consistency_verified" on their source

Type 3: Topic Expansion Decay

Pattern: You're cited for the narrow entity/topic. As the topic expands semantically, the model finds your content too narrow and routes to broader sources.

Cause: User questions evolve and become more specific. Your content was created for general audience; it doesn't have the depth the model needs for synthesized answers to narrowed queries.

Timeline: 2–4 weeks (parallels seasonal query shifts)

Forensic Signal:

  • CITE LEDGER shows you cited in broad queries but excluded from narrow variants
  • Authority stays stable, but semantic fit score drops
  • Model routes to more specialized sources that answer the narrowed query

Recovery Strategies by Decay Type

For Authority Reassignment Decay

Goal: Reclaim entity association before it solidifies.

  • Speed: Act within 7–10 days of competitor emergence
  • Action: Publish original research or data that competitors must cite
  • Evidence Signal: Get cited by the competitor (re-establishes you as source authority)
  • Timeline to Recovery: 3–4 weeks (if competitor citations are successful)

For Conflicting Information Decay

Goal: Resolve the conflict before models deprioritize you further.

  • Speed: Act immediately (decay is faster for this type)
  • Action: Update content to align with authoritative source consensus; add citations to those sources
  • Evidence Signal: CITE LEDGER shows semantic inconsistency resolved in re-scan
  • Timeline to Recovery: 1–2 weeks (faster, model respects corrections)

For Topic Expansion Decay

Goal: Expand content depth to match query evolution.

  • Speed: Proactive (expand before decay accelerates)
  • Action: Add deeper subsections addressing narrowed query variants; add FAQ blocks and schema
  • Evidence Signal: CITE LEDGER shows improved semantic fit score; synthesis inclusion increases
  • Timeline to Recovery: 2–4 weeks (parallels query seasonality cycles)

The Core Insight: Decay is Measurable Reputation Degradation

Traditional SEO treats all ranking losses the same. Model decay requires different diagnosis because it's driven by competing authority claims and model reasoning reassignment, not just algorithm updates.

The entities that maintain citation presence are those that detect decay early (within 7 days) and respond with original evidence or semantic resolution.

The ones that disappear ignored decay signals and let competitors establish authority unchallenged.
