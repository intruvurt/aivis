What BRAG Grading Means

BRAG stands for Based-Retrieval-Auditable-Grading. Every finding in an AiVIS report carries a BRAG identifier; a stable reference tied to a verifiable element on the live page being audited.

If a finding cannot be proven from the page; it does not make it into the score. This is the core commitment that separates AiVIS from generalized SEO checkers or assumption-based visibility estimates.

What AI Answer Engines Actually Look For

ChatGPT, Perplexity AI, Google AI Overviews, and Claude do not rank pages. They extract from them. Four structural signal categories determine whether a page gets cited or ignored.

01 / Structured Meaning

Schema markup, clear entity relationships, and well-defined content hierarchy. AI systems need to know what your page is about without ambiguity.

02 / Semantic Clarity

Coherent explanations, contextual depth, and unambiguous intent. Not just keywords; the content needs to stand on its own as a self-contained answer.

03 / Trust Signals

Author presence, consistent branding, domain-level security, outbound sources, and update freshness. AI systems deprioritize pages that lack these anchors.

04 / Freshness and Activity

Static content fades in AI citation priority. Active, updated pages receive higher confidence scores and more frequent citation in AI-generated responses.
