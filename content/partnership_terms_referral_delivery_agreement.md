# Referral and Delivery Partnership Terms

**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Party A:** [Your Full Name or Business Name]  
**Party B:** [Developer Company Full Legal Name]  

This Referral and Delivery Partnership Terms document sets out the working agreement between Party A and Party B regarding client acquisition, project closing, development delivery, payment handling, and protection of both parties.

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this agreement is to define how Party A and Party B will work together on client projects where Party A sources the client and Party B delivers the development work.

## 2. Roles of Each Party

### 2.1 Party A
Party A is responsible for:
- Sourcing and introducing potential clients
- Handling or supporting sales conversations where needed
- Managing client communication before project acceptance where applicable
- Receiving client payments unless otherwise agreed in writing
- Retaining the agreed commission amount before forwarding Party B's share

### 2.2 Party B
Party B is responsible for:
- Estimating project scope, technical feasibility, timelines, and pricing input where needed
- Handling development, implementation, testing, and delivery of the agreed project scope
- Communicating technical requirements, blockers, and delivery milestones in a timely manner
- Delivering work to a commercially reasonable standard based on the agreed scope

## 3. Lead Ownership

A lead will be considered owned by Party A if Party A was the first party to introduce the client to Party B, whether through direct introduction, email, message, meeting, or other documented communication.

If there is a dispute over lead ownership, the earliest verifiable written record of introduction will control.

## 4. Closing Responsibility

Client closing may be handled by Party A, Party B, or both parties together depending on the deal.

Regardless of who closes the client, if the lead originated from Party A, the compensation structure in this agreement will apply.

## 5. Compensation Structure

For every client introduced by Party A that becomes a signed and paid project, Party A will retain **10 percent of the total agreed project value** as commission.

Party B will receive **90 percent of the total agreed project value**.

The 10 percent commission applies to:
- Initial project value
- Approved scope expansions
- Approved upsells directly related to the same project or engagement

Unless otherwise agreed in writing, the commission is calculated on gross client payments actually received.

## 6. Payment Handling

### 6.1 Standard Structure
Party A will receive payment from the client and will forward Party B's 90 percent share while retaining Party A's 10 percent share.

### 6.2 Recommended Milestone Payment Structure
Where possible, payments should be collected in milestones rather than only at final delivery. In that case, Party A will retain 10 percent from each payment milestone received and forward 90 percent of that milestone amount to Party B.

### 6.3 Payment Timing
Party B's share will be sent within **[insert number] business days** after Party A receives cleared funds from the client for the relevant project payment or milestone.

## 7. Scope and Delivery

Before development begins, both parties should have written agreement on:
- Project scope
- Deliverables
- Timeline or milestone schedule
- Total project price
- Any assumptions, exclusions, or dependencies

Party B is responsible for delivering the scope that was agreed with the client. Any work outside the approved scope must be separately approved and priced.

## 8. Client Relationship and Representation

The client may be presented as working with Party A, Party B, or both parties depending on what is agreed for that project.

Unless otherwise agreed in writing:
- Party A owns the client relationship from an acquisition standpoint
- Party B acts as the delivery partner
- Party B will not bypass Party A to contract directly with a client introduced by Party A during the term of this agreement and for **[12] months** after the last active project involving that client

## 9. Non Circumvention

Party B agrees not to bypass, solicit, or directly contract with any client introduced by Party A outside this agreement without Party A's written consent.

If Party B directly contracts with a client introduced by Party A during the protected period, Party A will remain entitled to the 10 percent commission on all resulting work from that client during that period.

## 10. Refunds, Disputes, and Chargebacks

If a client requests a refund, initiates a dispute, or files a chargeback, both parties will cooperate in good faith to resolve the issue.

Unless the refund or dispute was caused by Party A's misconduct, misrepresentation, or payment handling failure, Party B will remain responsible for the delivery quality and any delivery-related issue that caused the dispute.

If funds must be returned to the client due to delivery failure by Party B, both parties will discuss a fair allocation of loss based on the reason for the refund and the amount already paid out.

## 11. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to keep private all confidential business, client, technical, pricing, and commercial information shared under this agreement, except where disclosure is required by law or necessary to deliver the project.

## 12. Independent Contractors

The parties are acting as independent contractors. This agreement does not create an employer-employee relationship, legal partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship beyond the limited commercial arrangement described here.

## 13. Limitation of Responsibility

Each party is responsible for its own actions, representations, taxes, compliance, and operational obligations.

Party A is not liable for technical delivery failures caused by Party B. Party B is not liable for client acquisition promises or commercial representations made by Party A that were not approved by Party B.

## 14. Term and Termination

This agreement starts on the Effective Date and continues until ended by either party through written notice.

Termination will not affect:
- Payment obligations already earned
- Existing live projects already accepted before termination
- Protected client relationships under the non circumvention clause during the stated protected period

## 15. Governing Law

This agreement will be governed by the laws of **[Insert State or Country]**, unless both parties agree otherwise in writing.

## 16. Entire Understanding

This document represents the current understanding between the parties regarding the subject matter above. Any changes must be made in writing and acknowledged by both parties.
