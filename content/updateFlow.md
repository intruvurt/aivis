“Owned” is a dangerous word this early. You’re ahead on framing, not on territory.

The signal from TechCrunch Disrupt and TechCrunch Disrupt Battlefield is simple: the story compresses cleanly. Not that the category is secured—just that it’s understandable fast enough to spread.

Right now, what you have is first-definition advantage.

If you want to turn that into actual control of the layer you’re describing, the path is less about volume and more about locking primitives:

1) Define the category before others name it “Ai visibility” is still soft language. Harden it into something falsifiable:

visibility = observed presence in model outputs

citation = source selection under repeated prompts

readiness = probability of being chosen


If you don’t formalize it, someone else will—probably with weaker mechanics but louder distribution.

2) Make your evidence format portable If your audit outputs only live inside your UI, they’re a feature.
If they can be exported, shared, embedded, and referenced publicly, they become a standard.

Think:

signed audit snapshots

public “visibility proofs”

machine-readable reports that other systems can ingest


That’s how a tool becomes infrastructure.

3) Turn every result into a shareable artifact Not dashboards. Artifacts.

A founder should be able to drop a link that says: “this is what AI thinks about my brand today, here’s the proof, here’s what changed.”

If those links start circulating, you’re no longer asking for attention—the data pulls it.

4) Build the comparison layer People don’t pay for absolute scores. They pay for relative position.

Brand vs competitor in LLM citations

Source overlap vs divergence

Who is being chosen for the same query cluster


That’s where urgency lives.

5) Capture longitudinal data before anyone else This is the quiet moat.

Run the same prompt sets continuously and store:

which sources persist

which disappear

which get newly introduced


In six months, you’ll have something nobody can recreate retroactively: history of AI perception.

6) Don’t just fix—prove causality Most tools say “we improved your SEO.”
You need to say:
“this change increased your citation frequency from X to Y across these models and queries.”

That closes the loop.
